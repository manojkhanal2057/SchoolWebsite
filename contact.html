We offer two programmes — a nurturing Boarding School for Classes 1–5 and an excellent Government-affiliated School for Classes 6–10. Apply today and secure your child's bright future.

Residential Programme
Boarding School
A safe, nurturing residential environment for young learners in Classes 1–5 with 24/7 care, structured routines, and holistic development.
  • Classes 1 to 5 (Age 5–12 years)
  • Full board – meals, accommodation & laundry
  • Supervised study & evening coaching
  • Trained house parents & 24/7 medical support
  • Regular parent communication & open days
  • Scholarship available for deserving students

Financial Support Available

We believe no student should miss out on quality education due to financial constraints. Scholarship and fee waiver programmes are available.

Need-Based AssistanceFor economically disadvantaged families. Evaluated on a case-by-case basis by the school committee.
Government ScholarshipDalit, Janajati & girls' scholarship available through the Government of Nepal for eligible students.
Help Centre

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common questions from parents and prospective students. Still have a question? Contact us directly.

Fill out the enquiry form (online or from office) → bring required documents → attend a brief interaction session with your child → receive your admission confirmation letter. The whole process typically takes 2–3 days. Limited boarding seats are available, so we recommend applying early.

Boarding fees cover accommodation, three nutritious meals and snacks per day, evening study supervision, basic medical care, laundry service, and access to all school facilities. Fees can be paid in two instalments (first and second term). Contact the office for the exact current fee schedule.

Yes. Our secondary school (Classes 6–10) is fully affiliated with the Government of Nepal and follows the National Examinations Board (NEB) curriculum. Students sit for the official SEE (Secondary Education Examination) in Class 10 and receive nationally recognised certificates.

Yes. We offer merit scholarships (for students scoring 80%+), need-based assistance for economically disadvantaged families, and government scholarships for Dalit, Janajati, and girl students as per Nepal government policy. Applications are reviewed individually — contact the office for the current application process.

Boarding students can make supervised phone/video calls to their parents daily during the designated evening call time (8:00 PM – 9:00 PM). Parents receive weekly SMS/WhatsApp updates and monthly written progress reports. There are also formal parent visiting days each month and termly parent-teacher meetings.

We take student safety extremely seriously. The boarding facility is monitored with 24/7 CCTV cameras, controlled entry/exit gates, and trained residential staff on night duty. We have a medical room with first-aid supplies, emergency protocols, and direct links to the nearest hospital. No student may leave campus without written parental permission.

We offer a wide range of activities including football, cricket, volleyball, athletics, badminton, as well as music, dance, drama, art & craft, science club, debate society, and community service. Co-curricular activities are scheduled into the timetable to ensure all students can participate — not just academically strong students.

The academic year begins on Baisakh 1 (mid-April) as per the Nepali calendar. Admissions for the new academic year typically open in Chaitra (March) and close when seats are filled. We strongly recommend applying at least 1–2 months before the academic year starts. Boarding seats fill up quickly, so early application is advised.
